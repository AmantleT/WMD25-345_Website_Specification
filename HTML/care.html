Instrument Care

Proper care of musical instruments is necessary in order to maintain sound quality and extend thier lifespan.

String Instruments

Plucked Instruments

Pluked instruments should be regularly cleaned to remove oils and dirt that transfered on the string during playing. Strings may need to be replaced once they wear out. The body of the instrument should be kept clean and stored away when not being used. Avoid exposing the instruments to moisture or direct sunlight as these can affect the sound and structure.

Bowed Instruments

These should be handled carefully to avoid damaging the strings and the bow. After use/ playing the cello, violin or or any other bowed instrument the hairs of the bow must be lossened to maintain quality. The strings must be cleaned to remove rosin build-up which should be done using a soft cloth.They should be stored away from humidity and extreme temperatures to avoid the wood being damaged

Hammered Instruments

A stable humidity must be maintained in order to avoid the wook from cracking and strings from rusting. Inatruments must be kept away from high temperatures and direct sunlight and wipe strings to prevent rust and oil buildup.

Woodwind Instruments

Flute

Flutes should be cleaned after each use with a cleaning rod and cloth to remove moisture fron the inside.

Reeds

The instrument must be cleaned after use to remove moisture from the indside. The reed/reeds should be removed and stored properly to prevent damage, the mouthpiece should be cleaned regularly and should be handled to prevent damaging the keys.
